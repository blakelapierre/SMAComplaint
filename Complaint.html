Background

I was an employee of Software Marketing Associates, Inc. from September 2007 to June 15, 2012. SMA and I had an extremely simple contract, including an employer match on my 401 (k) contributions up to 6% of my annual salary, starting after the first year of our relationship. Our contract did not specify an exact schedule for the distribution of the matching funds, but over the period of our contract SMA made employer matching contributions roughly every three months. The contributions were equal to exactly the amounts I contributed to my Account for a given quarter of the Plan's fiscal year. On some occasions, the Administrator personally notified myself and other employees that the "quarterly match" had been contributed to Participant Accounts. On June 18, 2012 the Administrator removed assets from my Account. The Administrator never notified me of this action (either before or after); it was only discovered later when I was reviewing my Account statement. Note: The contributions worksheet, linked above, was provided by the Administrator and makes an inaccurate statement regarding the vesting status of my Account. Contrary to this worksheet, my Account is fully vested. The Administrator does not dispute the vesting status of my Account and its vesting status is not related to this Complaint, other than through this Note.

The Administrator's Claims

The Administrator now makes many dubious and contradictory claims, including that at least two (2) of the thirteen (13) employer matching contributions made to my Account were actually "advances". The Administrator claims that I had to be employed on a specific day of the year (June 30) to be "eligible for the discretionary match"; that SMA "had to take the advances back because [I was] not eligible to receive them"; and that "recovery of [my] pre-payments is compulsory - not optional". The Administrator first claimed that the "advances" are made "as a courtesy" to employees, but later claimed that the "partial payments" are actually made "solely for [their] convenience in smoothing out [their] cash flow", if "[their] profits permit". The Administrator claims that I "misunderst[ood]" their benefits, and asserts that it is a fact that I was not "unfairly cheated out of a benefit that [I] thought [I] earned". The Administrator claims that it made a "mistake of fact" when it "anticipat[ed] that [I] would still be an employee at 06/30/2012". The Administrator had no basis, and provides no rationale, for its belief that it would be a fact that I would be an employee of SMA on June 30, 2012. SMA's "Disclaimer", distributed by the Administrator, states, "[t]he basic employment relationship at SMA is of an at-will nature and there is no specified length or guarantee of employment". No type of relationship other than the basic employment relationship was offered to me by SMA. The Administrator's claims are an appeal to the authority of documents that were first made known and distributed to me fully one (1) month AFTER the Administrator injured my Account. I will show that the Administrator selectively picks clauses from these documents to support its position while continually and blatantly misrepresenting and disregarding the balance of the documents.

The Administrator Disregards Fact and Evidence

The Administrator's claims are in direct contradiction with the contract between myself and SMA, as well as in direct contradiction with every representation that the Administrator, Trustees, SMA or any employee of SMA ever made to me or to other former and current employees of SMA, as well as in direct contradiction with a document distributed by the Administrator that explicitly states "SMA does not provide pay advances on unearned wages to associates." This document was distributed to me by the Administrator just five (5) months prior to the Administrator making these claims. I have spoken to a number of current and former associates of SMA, including multiple Vice Presidents; none were aware that the employer matching contributions were "advances" or that there was only a single day of the year that one could be employed in order to not have their "advances" taken back.
